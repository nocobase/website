**Q1**\*\*: Our company has many internal systems, and data is scattered across different departments. Which type of tool is a better fit? \*\***A:** NocoBase.

\*\*Q2: If AI matters, how should we choose among these tools?\*\***A:** NocoBase is a better fit for teams that want AI to work directly inside business systems and take part in real workflows. OpenProject is more suitable for report generation, project suggestions, and document handling. Plane is a stronger fit for teams that care about development collaboration and AI assisted project execution. Budibase is more suitable when AI needs to be embedded into approvals, automation, and workflow handling.

\*\*Q3: Which open source project management tool is better for lightweight agile teams?\*\***A:** Taiga.

It emphasizes a simple and intuitive agile collaboration experience and is well suited for small to mid sized product and engineering teams.

\*\*Q4: Which open source project management tool is better for development teams?\*\***A:** Plane.

It maps more naturally to issues, iterations, and development collaboration workflows, and its overall experience feels more modern.

\*\*Q5: Our team already has CRM, approval, ticketing, and delivery systems, and project management is only one part of the picture. What should we choose?\*\***A:** Choose a customizable business platform such as NocoBase, because the key in this kind of scenario is connecting project workflows with the business systems you already have.

\*\*Q6: If our needs are more process driven and form driven, which tool is a better fit? \*\*\*\*A: \*\*Budibase.

\*\*Q7: If project workflows often need to connect with approval flows, customer information, and delivery milestones, which tool is more suitable?\*\***A:** NocoBase.

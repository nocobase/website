## What Problems Can a Training Tracker Solve?

As the number of training courses grows, course catalogs, calendar schedules, learner enrollments, and completion records can easily become scattered across spreadsheets or multiple tools. HR and training teams then struggle to see which courses are progressing well, which courses have low completion rates, and which learners are already overdue.

The course catalog helps centralize compliance, leadership, onboarding, product, safety, and technical courses. Each course can record its course code, delivery format, duration, credits, difficulty, average completion rate, enrollment count, and owner, making it easier for teams to compare execution across different courses.

The training calendar is useful for managing course sessions. Teams can view schedules by month, week, or day, such as new employee onboarding, anti-harassment training, Kubernetes deep-dive sessions, office safety, and remote workstation training, reducing scheduling conflicts and missed sessions.

AI training analysis reports can turn course, session, and enrollment data into management-friendly insights. Total sessions, total seats, occupied seats, utilization rate, remaining seats, monthly enrollment patterns, and performance by delivery format can all be summarized in a report.

## Key Features

### Training Overview Dashboard

* **Active course count:** Shows the number of currently running courses, such as 12 active courses.

### Course Catalog Management

* **Course cards:** Display course name, course code, category, delivery format, duration, and credits.
* **Category management:** Supports categories such as compliance, leadership, onboarding, product, safety, and technical training.
* **Completion rate display:** Shows the average completion rate for each course so teams can evaluate progress.
* **Enrollment count:** Shows the current number of enrollments for each course, such as 6 or 8 learners.
* **Owner information:** Displays the course owner on each card for easier communication and follow-up.

### Training Calendar Scheduling

* **Monthly view:** Shows course schedules by month for overall planning and resource allocation.
* **Weekly view:** Shows training sessions by time slot, making it easier to detect conflicts.
* **Daily view:** Provides a closer look at the training plan for a specific day.
* **Session display:** The calendar can show course names and time ranges.
* **Schedule maintenance:** Supports filtering, refreshing, and adding new training sessions.

### Course Detail Management

* **Basic information:** Shows the course title, course code, and course summary.
* **Attributes:** Shows category, delivery format, level, duration, and credits.
* **Status information:** Records whether the course is active.
* **Metadata:** Displays creation time and update time to help track course maintenance.
* **Edit entry:** Users can open editing from the detail page to keep course information updated.

### AI Training Analysis Reports

* **Session summary:** AI can summarize the number of training sessions, such as 21 sessions.
* **Seat analysis:** Reports can analyze total seats, occupied seats, remaining seats, and utilization rate.
* **Completion status:** Summarizes learner statuses such as completed, in progress, and pending.
* **Schedule analysis:** Compares enrollment performance across months, delivery formats, and course types.
* **Report export:** Supports Markdown, HTML, and PDF output for reporting and archiving.
